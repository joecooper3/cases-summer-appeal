The mission of the Center for Alternative Sentencing and Employment Services (CASES) is to increase public safety through innovative services that reduce crime and incarceration, improve behavioral health, promote recovery and rehabilitation, and create opportunities for success in the community.

8,402

8,402

This is the most recent daily count announced by New York City officials of people currently detained at Rikers Island. 8,402. That's down from 200,000+ detained the facility back in the 1980s, before CASES was established. That 8,402 is also down more than a thousand from 9,500 at the end of 2017.

3,357

3,357

In the past year, CASES diverted 3,357 people from detention and incarceration. 3,357. That's youth age 12 to 24 and men and women including several hundred with serious mental illness. Every day, CASES’ programs engage these individuals in behavioral health, education, employment, family, housing, and peer services at our office locations in four boroughs.

$26 million

$26 million

That’s roughly the cost of operating Rikers Island for 7 days, according to a NYC Comptroller report last November stating the daily cost of operating the jail is about $3.73 million. At this rate, the cost of operating Rikers Island for 1 week—$26 million—would more than pay for 1 year of operating CASES, which has a current annual budget of about $24 million.

24

24

That’s the number of programs CASES will operate this year, providing interventions across all stages of the criminal justice system, with programs to prevent a young person’s initial arrest; alternatives to bail and pretrial detention; alternatives to jail and prison; and neighborhood-based services to support reentry. CASES will also continue to provide a range of behavioral health services including at our Harlem mental health clinic and via mobile treatment teams annually making about 20,000 clients visits in neighborhoods citywide.

We seek justice and healing for all our clients, and we work one case at a time in order to build on individual strengths and goals while addressing unique risks and needs. While CASES works closely with government agencies to improve the system from within, we also develop and implement innovative strategies to address emerging needs and gaps not yet recognized by public agencies. To create these innovative solutions to some of the most difficult public safety and public health challenges, CASES relies on private support including foundation and corporate grants and individual donations.
